Problem

To whom it may concern

Staring into the dark abyss of a empty fridge. Feeling the smell of a rotten fruit. The uneasy feeling of throwing out food that could have been eaten, just if you had taken the time to plan. Or use it in a recipe.

All these feelings and emotions all relate to the findings of recent research that show that people between the ages of 20 and 30, recent mothers and people with a lot of money, throw a lot of perfectly good food.

The problem

Producing food costs resources. These resources include land, water, energy, soil, seeds and other inputs. When we produce food that never gets eaten, these resources are wasted. The waste of these recourses increases the greenhouse gas in vain. The reduction in food waste would also help us achieve the UN sustainability goal of zero hunger.

The problem of food loss lies in all stages of “the life cycle of food”. These include production, processing, retailing and consumption. In low-income countries, most of the food is wasted during the production stage. In higher income countries a lot of the food is wasted at the consumption stage. The possible reason for this is that higher income countries take food for granted, and they have generally lower respect for food.

This is what the UN says:

"Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns" includes amongst its objectives to "halve per capita global food waste at the retail and consumer level, and reduce food losses along production and supply chains by 2030".

What can you do to help us together to reach the UN sustainability goal?

We believe that awareness and a greater understanding of the effects of food waste, will increase the respect we have for food. Optimat does this by making it easier for you to keep track of your food. You can read more about this in our solution tab.

I am a recent parent

"With a child, there is a lot of things to think about and different variety of food that I have to make. Because of this, I lose track of time and the things I have in my fridge."

I am between 20 and 30

"Being young, I often eat outside and forget that I have food in my fridge. Additionally, I don’t think too much about my spending, but it always hurts at the end of the month."

I am wealthy

"Money is not my real motivation for taking more responsibility towards my food consumption. Because of this, I would rather think about how my habits can help the environment."

Optimat can help with all of the above!

By helping you manage your food usage and dates of expiry, you can help support the UN goals by making micro changes to your habits.

Over time, this can have a big impact on both your wallet, the environment and also the society. Less waste is always better!
